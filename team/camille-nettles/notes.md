### Phase 2 — Badge + Lanyard System (May 9–14)

Badges are small but high-touch — every speaker and executive will be wearing one all day. The system needs to work across five tiers: Program Lead (Maigh Houlihan), Executives ×4, Ambassadors ×17, Site Leads ×3.

**How to approach it:**

1. **Establish the badge hierarchy first** — each tier should feel distinct but clearly part of the same system. Think about how color, type size, or a visual element signals the difference between an ambassador and an executive.
2. **Design front and back** — front is identity (name, title, tier), back is utility (WiFi, venue map, QR code). Jeff Hampton is writing the back copy — coordinate with him on space constraints.
3. **Lanyard design** — custom print, needs to complement but not compete with the badge. A simple repeating pattern or wordmark works well.
4. **Check vendor specs early** — badge size (standard is 3.5" × 4"), bleed, color mode, whether they print double-sided. Get this from Denise before you finalize layouts.

**Recommended tools:**

- **Adobe Illustrator** — badge and lanyard layout. One master file with artboards for each tier.
- **Adobe Firefly** — texture and pattern exploration for lanyard design
- **Canva** (optional) — useful for quick badge mockups if you want to test hierarchy before committing in Illustrator

### Phase 3 — Tier 1 Swag + Tier 3 Packaging (May 14–22)

Four Tier 1 items going to 830 people each. These need to feel premium and intentional, not afterthought merch.

**Sticker (D5-01):**
- Bold, simple, works at 2–3 inches. Die-cut shapes read better than rectangles.
- Check Sticker Mule specs before designing — they have templates.
- Holographic or foil finish options worth exploring given the AI theme.

**Notebook (D5-02):**
- Confirm cover print area with Denise's vendor. Usually front cover only.
- Keep the design clean — notebooks get used, so the design lives in the real world.

**Enamel pin (D5-04):**
- Enamel pins are limited to flat color fills — no gradients. Design accordingly.
- Standard size is 1–1.5 inches. Keep shapes simple and recognizable at that scale.
- Minimum order quantities can be high — confirm with Denise before designing.

**Tier 3 packaging (D5-09):**
- This is the premium unboxing experience for VIPs. Think about the reveal — what does someone see when they open the box?
- Coordinate with Denise on what's going inside before designing the packaging.

**Recommended tools:**

- **Adobe Illustrator** — all swag artwork. Vector-only for print.
- **Adobe Firefly** — texture, pattern, and background exploration
- **Sticker Mule template library** — free die-cut and shape templates for sticker design
- **Pinterest** — swag and packaging inspiration. Search "event swag packaging" and "enamel pin design"

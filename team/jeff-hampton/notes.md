### Task 1 — Logo Usage Guide (by May 14)

This document tells the whole team and any future contributors how to use the Project AIR logo correctly. It needs to exist before anyone starts adapting the logo for swag, PPT, or digital.

**How to approach it:**

1. **Wait for the final logo from Michael** (target May 9) — you cannot write usage rules until the asset exists
2. Cover the basics: minimum size, clear space, approved color variations (full color, white, black), what backgrounds it can go on, what it cannot do (stretch, rotate, recolor, add effects)
3. Keep it short — one page if possible, two max. Nobody reads a 10-page brand bible for an internal event.
4. Write it in plain language. "Always leave at least this much space around the logo" is better than "maintain a minimum exclusion zone equal to the cap height of the logotype."

**Recommended tools:**

- **Google Docs** — write the rules here, Camille or Lee drops it into the final designed doc
- **Gemini or Claude** — useful for drafting the do/don't language if you want to iterate quickly

### Task 3 — AI Prompt Card Copy (by May 14)

This one matters. 835 people are going home with this card. For most of them, it might be the first time they actually try an AI tool. The copy needs to be welcoming, fun, and make someone feel like they can do this.

**How to approach it:**

1. **Read Mark's positioning brief first** — the tone of this card lives inside the brand voice he establishes
2. **Think about the person holding it** — a meteorologist, an HR manager, a salesperson, a lawyer. Most have never typed a prompt. The card should feel like a friendly nudge, not a tutorial.
3. **Structure:** A short intro line (1 sentence), then 5-8 prompt starters they can actually use. Make the prompts specific and useful, not generic. "Summarize this meeting in 3 bullet points" is better than "Help me be more productive."
4. **Leave room for the AI theme** — this is an AI Day card, lean into it. A bit of wit is welcome.
5. **Write long, then cut** — draft 15-20 prompts and cut to the best 5-8. The editing is where the card gets good.

**Recommended tools:**

- **Gemini** — use it to generate prompt ideas, then edit them. Meta, but appropriate.
- **Google Flow** — if you want to build a rapid iteration workflow: input a prompt theme, output 10 variations, pick the best
- **Claude** — strong at tone calibration. Paste your draft and ask it to make it sound more like a festival program than a user manual.

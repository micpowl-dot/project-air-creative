### Task 2 — Vendor Confirmation (by May 16)

You need two vendors locked: large-format print and swag/merchandise.

**How to approach it — Print vendor:**

1. You need a vendor that can handle large-format poster printing at event quality
2. Get quotes from at least 2 vendors before committing
3. Key specs to ask for: largest print size available, turnaround time, bleed requirements, file format (PDF/TIFF), CMYK vs RGB submission
4. Confirm they can deliver to Brookhaven HQ (Atlanta) and ask about shipping to Andover, Seoul, and New York

**How to approach it — Swag vendor:**

1. Tier 1 (×830): stickers, notebooks, enamel pins, AI prompt cards — need a vendor with volume capability
2. Tier 2 (~25): t-shirts/hoodies, tote bags — could be same vendor or separate
3. Key questions: minimum order quantities, production lead times, can they do custom enamel pins, what are the artwork specs
4. Build in at least 10 business days for production + shipping from order date

**Recommended tools:**

- **Sticker Mule** — reliable for stickers and enamel pins at volume, fast turnaround
- **CustomInk or Printful** — t-shirts and totes
- **4imprint or Cimpress** — full swag packages if you want one vendor for everything
- **Printingforless.com or Moo** — premium large-format and event print

### Task 3 — Production Trafficking (May 27 onward)

From May 27, your job is moving final approved files from the design team to vendors and tracking everything to the door.

**How to approach it:**

1. Set up a simple tracking sheet (Google Sheets works fine) — one row per deliverable, columns for: file sent, vendor confirmed receipt, production start, ship date, delivery confirmed
2. Brief the design team on print specs before they finalize files — bleed, CMYK, resolution, file format. Catching a wrong color profile before the file goes to print saves days.
3. Build a checklist for each vendor submission: correct file format, correct dimensions, correct color profile, Mark's approval confirmed
4. Once files are sent, follow up with vendors within 24 hours to confirm receipt and get a production timeline in writing

**Recommended tools:**

- **Google Sheets** — production tracker. Simple, shareable, everyone can see status.
- **Slack** — keep a #project-air-production channel for vendor updates and file handoffs
- **Dropbox or Google Drive** — send large print files via shared link, not email attachment

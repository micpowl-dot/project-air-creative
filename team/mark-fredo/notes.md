### Task 2 — Positioning/Messaging Brief (May 9)

This is the most important thing you write on this project. It tells the whole team what Project AIR feels like in words — and those words become poster headlines, badge copy, prompt card language, and the PPT narrative.

**How to approach it:**

1. **Start with the audience** — 835 people, most of whom have never seriously used AI. The brief needs to speak to curiosity and possibility, not technical capability.
2. **Write the feeling first** — before messaging pillars or taglines, write 2-3 sentences describing how someone should feel walking into AI Day. That becomes your north star.
3. **Define what we are and what we are not** — SXSW meets Coachella, yes. Cold dark-mode futurism, no. Put that in writing so the design team has guardrails.
4. **Write 3-5 headline directions** — short, punchy, usable. Jeff will riff on these for copy. Michael will pull from them for poster headlines.

**Recommended tools:**

- **ChatGPT or Claude** — paste your rough thinking and ask it to sharpen the language. Great for headline iteration.
- **Google Docs** — write the brief here, share the link in Slack, drop it in your team folder here too.
- **Competitor/inspiration reference** — look at SXSW, TED, and Cannes Lions comms for tone benchmarks. Not to copy, but to know what register you're writing in.

**Tool:** PDF review, Slack feedback, or annotate directly in Figma if Lee is working there.

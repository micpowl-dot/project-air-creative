# Decision Handoff Process

> How creative direction gets from Michael's head to the team's files.

---

## The Problem This Solves

Decisions get made in conversation — in meetings, in reviews, in sessions with Claude. If they only live in someone's memory or a chat log, they create gaps. A designer opens their files and has no idea what changed, what was approved, or what direction to take. This process closes that gap.

---

## How It Works

Every time a meaningful creative decision is made — direction approved, approach changed, reference locked in — it follows this path:

```
Decision made
     │
     ▼
Decision log created in decisions/
(use 000-template.md, name it DEC-00X-short-title.md)
     │
     ▼
Relevant team member notes updated
(add a "Current Direction" block at the top of their notes.md)
     │
     ▼
Brand guidelines updated if it affects the visual system
(docs/brand-guidelines.md)
```

---

## What Goes in a Decision Log

Not every small call needs a log. Log it when:
- A creative direction is set or changed
- A deliverable approach is approved
- A reference is adopted as a standard
- A constraint is added or removed

Skip it for: typo fixes, file naming, scheduling changes (those go in _NEXT_STEPS.md).

---

## What Goes in Team Notes

Every designer's `notes.md` should have a **Current Direction** block at the top. This is a 4-5 bullet plain-English summary of where things stand right now. It gets updated every time a decision log is created.

The rule: if someone opens their notes.md cold — no meeting, no Slack, no context — they should be able to understand what they're building and where to find the details.

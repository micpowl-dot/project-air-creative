## Creative Direction

### The Vibe: SXSW Meets Coachella

Project AIR is the effort driving AI adoption across The Weather Company. AI Day is the centerpiece event: a mandatory, full-company experience for approximately 830 people. But mandatory does not mean it should feel mandatory. The creative direction is festival: the energy of Coachella, the intellectual curiosity of SXSW. Participants should walk into Brookhaven and know within ten seconds that this is not a normal corporate event.

This is not a tech conference. The audience includes meteorologists, salespeople, finance, HR, legal, and engineering. Most have never sent an AI prompt. The design should feel warm, inviting, and slightly irreverent. Think bold color, large-scale type, movement, texture. Not cold. Not dark-mode. Not blue-glow futurism.

The creative direction is yours to define. We are giving you the vibe, the deliverables, and the constraints. How you get there is your call.

## Deliverable 1 — Speaker Spotlight Posters

Festival-style posters spotlighting the people leading AI Day. These should look like artist lineup posters, not corporate headshot slides.

### Who to Feature

**The stars of AI Day are the 17 Ambassadors** who are leading the sessions. These are internal employees from across the company who volunteered, trained, and are running the day. They are the face of the event. Their posters should be the most visible, the most prominent, and the most numerous.

Executive speakers also get poster treatments, but the Ambassadors come first in placement, scale, and emphasis:

- 17 Ambassadors: internal session leads across all functions (names and photos TBD)
- Rohit Agarwal, CEO (Opening keynote)
- Matthew Drooker, CTO (Close, commitment ritual)
- Brian O'Neil, SVP AI and Platform Engineering (AI Jeopardy host)
- Dan Marguiles, CIO (AI Club, Andover)

### Format and Placement

- Large-format prints for Brookhaven HQ: hallways, venue entrances, common areas
- Digital versions for screens and WorkVivo
- Scalable for social sharing on internal channels

### Creative Direction

Think music festival artist cards. Bold type, unexpected crops, saturated color. Each person gets a distinct treatment within the same system. The Ambassador posters are the headliners. Executive posters are supporting acts. Brian gets something playful (he is hosting a game show).

**Do not use:** corporate headshot grids, podium photos, clip art, stock photography, or anything that looks like it came from a PowerPoint template.

## Deliverable 2 — Lanyards and Presenter Badges

Physical lanyards and badges for AI Day presenters, Ambassadors, and program leads. These are the wearable identity of the event.

### Tiers

- Program Lead: Maigh Houlihan (distinct design, one of a kind)
- Executive Speakers: Rohit, Matthew, Brian, Dan (4)
- Ambassadors: 17 internal session leads (distinct from general participant badges)
- Site Leads: Andover, Birmingham, Seoul (3)

### Design Requirements

- Lanyard: branded, custom print. Not a blank lanyard with a clip-on insert.
- Badge: name, role, and a visual element that ties to the festival identity.
- Ambassador badges should be visibly different from general participant badges. These people are the face of the event and need to be findable in a crowd.
- Dual-sided badge recommended: front is the participant-facing design, back can include the wifi password, the Hallway Track map, or the session grid QR code.

### Creative Direction

Festival wristband energy, translated to a lanyard. The kind of thing people keep on their desk after the event because it looks good, not because they forgot to throw it away.

## Deliverable 3 — Project AIR Logo (Animated)

A logo for Project AIR: AI in Reach. This is the visual identity for the effort driving AI adoption across TWCo. It needs to work across all contexts: print, digital, screens, communications, and animated for video and screen content.

### Usage Contexts

- Warmup communications (email headers, The Drop posts, WorkVivo)
- AI Day event signage and environmental graphics
- Digital screens throughout Brookhaven HQ on event day
- Opening and closing video moments (Rohit's open, Matthew's close)
- Ongoing program identity: quarterly activations through March 2027
- Post-event reinforcement arc (Day 1, Day 7, Day 30 communications)

### Design Requirements

- Static version: works at all scales from email signature to large-format signage
- Animated version: 3–5 second loop suitable for video intros, screen savers, and digital signage
- Must work on light and dark backgrounds
- Must coexist with TWCo corporate branding without competing

### What to Know About the Name

Project AIR is the program name. AI in Reach can be included as a tagline or descriptor. The name was coined by Matthew Drooker and is the program's identity going forward through March 2027 and potentially beyond.

### Creative Direction

The animation should feel alive and kinetic. Think: something between a breath and a pulse. The word AIR gives natural direction: movement, lightness, atmosphere. Avoid mechanical or robotic animation. This is about humans working with AI, not about machines.

## Deliverable 4 — PowerPoint Template

A branded PowerPoint template for use across all AI Day and Project AIR presentations. This template will be used by the program lead, Ambassadors, and executive speakers. It needs to be flexible enough for strategy content, technical walkthroughs, and interactive session slides.

### Required Slide Layouts

- Title slide (event branding, speaker name, session title)
- Section divider (bold, full-bleed, minimal text)
- Content slide: text-heavy (for strategy and framing sessions)
- Content slide: image + text (for demos and walkthroughs)
- Content slide: two-column comparison
- Quote or callout slide (large type, single message)
- Blank slide with branded frame (for live demos and screen shares)
- Closing slide (next steps, contact, QR code)

### Design Requirements

- Consistent with the AI Day visual system (posters, badges, logo)
- Works in both 16:9 and 4:3 if feasible; 16:9 as priority
- Includes a speaker notes area formatted for readability
- Color palette and type choices should be documented on a "how to use this template" slide
- File delivered as .potx (template) and .pptx (editable example deck)

### Creative Direction

This template gets used by people who are not designers. It needs to look polished with minimal effort. Build in constraints that make it hard to produce an ugly slide: strong grid, limited font choices, pre-set color palette. Think keynote-quality defaults that work even when someone just types and hits present.

## Deliverable 5 — Swag and Premium Items

A tiered premium structure that rewards participation and creates visible social momentum throughout the day. The tiers are designed so that everyone leaves with something, presenters are recognized, and employees who go above and beyond earn something worth keeping.

### Tier Structure

| Tier | Who Gets It | Purpose |
|------|-------------|---------|
| **Tier 1** | Everyone (~830 participants) | Universal participation. Every person walks away with something branded. |
| **Tier 2** | Presenters: Ambassadors, executive speakers, site leads (~25) | Recognition for the people who built and ran the day. |
| **Tier 3** | Employees who go above and beyond: Bingo winners, standout contributions, AI Helped Me wall, exceptional engagement | Reward for visible effort. Creates social proof and FOMO that drives participation. |

### Tier 1: Everyone Gets

Ideas for items that are useful, branded, and low enough cost to distribute to ~830 people:

- Project AIR sticker (die-cut, high quality, laptop-worthy)
- Branded notebook (pocket-sized, clean design)
- AI prompt card (laminated, five universal prompts for any role)
- Custom enamel pin
- Branded pen or stylus

### Tier 2: Presenters

Items for the ~25 people who presented, facilitated, or led sessions. Should feel like a meaningful step up from Tier 1:

- Custom t-shirt or hoodie (AI Day branded, limited run)
- Premium water bottle or tumbler
- Branded wireless charging pad
- Custom tote bag (canvas, festival-style)
- Tier 2 items can include a Tier 1 item as part of the package

### Tier 3: Above and Beyond

Items for employees who earn recognition during the event through Bingo completion, AI Helped Me wall contributions, standout session participation, or other engagement mechanics TBD. These should be desirable enough that people actively try to earn them:

- Noise-cancelling earbuds or headphones
- Premium backpack or messenger bag
- High-end portable speaker
- Choice of item from a curated selection (via vendor portal)
- Custom jacket or vest (limited edition, event-year branded)

### Design Considerations

All items should tie visually to the AI Day identity system. The sticker and pin especially: these are the items that live on laptops and lanyards for months after the event. They are ongoing brand impressions. Make them good enough that people actually use them.

Tier 3 items should feel exclusive. Limited quantity, distinct packaging, visible at the event so other participants see them and want one. The social proof mechanic matters as much as the item itself.
